End-to-End Automatic Speech Recognition for Gujarati
Deepang Raval*, Vyom Pathak*, Muktan Patel*, Brijesh Bhatt
17th International Conference on Natural Language Processing
(ICON 2020) (ACL Anthology), December 2020
[Paper] / [Code] / [Oral Talk]

We invented a two tier post-processing technique to correct errors of an end-to-end ASR system for low-resource language (Gujarati). Firstly, we develop a novel multi-level (character as well as word level) language model for contraining the beam search decoding on the ASR output. On top of that, we develop a novel Spell Correction model based on multi-lingual BERT and a word-level langauge model. Finally, we provide a new and extensive analysis method to understand the errors made by the ASR system for the Gujarati Language.

mRNA Vaccine Degradation Prediction
Vyom Pathak*, Rahul Roy*
[Code] / [Slides]

We compared 2 methods to predict the degration of the mRNA Vaccine protein degradation. We investigate graph based as well as sequence-to-sequence model for the RNA structure on the Eterna Dataset. Our results shows that the graph based solution better represent the dataset thus giving better performance than the sequence-to-sequence model.
Image based Melanoma Classification using Semi-Supervised learning
Vyom Pathak*, Sanjana Rao*
[Code] / [Slides]

We present an ensemble of image-only convolutional neural network (CNN) models with different backbones and input sizes along with a self-supervised model to classify skin lesions. We use the Bootstrap your own latent (BYOL) model for self-supervision on the state-of-the-art image recognition models for performance improvements. With this, we show an improvement of 2% over the baselines.
Zero Shot Schema based Dialogue System
Vyom Pathak*, Amogh Mannekote*, Oluwapemisin Bandy-toyo*
[Code] / [Slides]

We have developed a Schema-based Dialogue System for Zero-Shot Task Transfer which enables designers to quickly and easily transfer the learned behaviors from related tasks/domains to new domains or tasks. We used zero-shot prompt-based Dialogue-GPT2 for the response generation part. We verified the viability of the system as a wire-framing technique through two user studies.
Common Literature Readibility Prediction
Vyom Pathak*, Muktan Patel*, Deepang Raval
[Post 1] / [Post 2]

We participated in Kaggle competition to predict the difficulty of the given paragraph as a regression score. We developed a novel 2D-Attention mechanism to boost our performance, along with finetuned several large language language models. The final model was an ensemble based on the Forward OOF method which helped us achieve Silver medal in the competition.
